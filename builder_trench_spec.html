2. Trench routing priorities

  1. Through flower beds wherever possible — easier digging, no lawn damage.
  2. Along path edges — under the first 200 mm of adjacent soil, invisible after re-turfing.
  3. Avoid lawn crossings — each one settles differently to the surrounding lawn and shows as a soft strip.
  4. Detour around tree roots — do not cut major roots; go around. Swing joints on the sprinklers absorb the reach.
  5. Route stays ~150–200 mm inside the lawn/bed edge — do not trench at the perimeter itself. Swing joints reach outward to the sprinklers.

3. Backfill profile

Shared trench (4 pipes)
  1. 50 mm sharp-sand bed (compacted)
  2. Lay pipes (Leo) — parallel, no stacking
  3. 100 mm sand surround over + around pipes
  4. Screened soil backfill to 250 mm below grade
  5. Blue "Water" warning tape — full trench width
  6. Screened soil in 150 mm lifts, tamp each lift
  7. Turf or topsoil finish
Solo lateral trench
  1. 30 mm sharp-sand bed
  2. Lay pipe (Leo)
  3. 50 mm sand surround
  4. Screened soil backfill to 200 mm
  5. Warning tape optional (drained each winter)
  6. Screened soil in 150 mm lifts, tamp
  7. Turf or topsoil finish
No sharp stones near pipes. MDPE is tough but not invulnerable. Sand surround is not optional — if offcut fines are unavailable, pick up a bag of sharp sand.

4. Swing-joint service pockets (at each sprinkler)

  • At every sprinkler position, dig a 150 mm-diameter vertical pocket from the trench up to grade.
  • Pocket is offset horizontally from the trench centreline toward the sprinkler head position (typically 150–200 mm off trench line).
  • Leo will install the swing joint in an S-shape through this pocket and screw on the pop-up at grade level.
  • Backfill the pocket with screened soil only — no sharp stones around the swing joint articulations.

5. Valve box bases (Box A / B / C)

Boxes must sit on a load-bearing pad that won't settle. The base needs to be built up tall enough that the box lid finishes flush with grade (max +10 mm proud, never below grade).

Grade ─────────────────────────────────────── 0 ┌────────────────────────┐ │ BOX LID │ ← at grade │ │ │ BOX (300 mm deep) │ │ │ └────────────────────────┘ ← box floor at –300 mm ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓ ← bricks (height-adjust, ~65–130 mm) · · · pea gravel · · · · ← 20–30 mm drainage ░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░ ← 10–20 mm fine-sand levelling bed ▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒ ← compacted Type 1, shuttered (fills over-dig) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ ← compacted pit floor
Layer recipe (bottom-up):
  1. Pit floor compacted — walk/stomp it before laying anything.
  2. Compacted Type 1 in 50 mm lifts, tamped hard with hammer face or fence-post end per lift. This is the bulk of the over-dig fill and the load-bearing foundation.
  3. Shutter the Type 1 zone with scrap timber/ply panels (see below) on sides where no pipe enters. Confines the Type 1 during compaction; can be left in place permanently.
  4. 10–20 mm fine sand bed to level the top of the Type 1.
  5. 20–30 mm pea gravel drainage layer.
  6. Bricks (or concrete pavers) on flat to bring the box floor to the right height. Adjust brick count per box to land the lid at grade.
  7. Box on bricks. Check spirit-level both directions before pipes connect.
Shuttering — where it goes:
  • Build a 3-sided "open U" or 2-sided "channel" shutter around each box's Type 1 zone — open on the side(s) where pipes enter.
  • BOX A and BOX B: pipes enter opposite sides → shutter the two remaining sides only (front + back).
  • BOX C: pipe enters one side (inlet) → shutter the other three sides.
  • Material: any scrap 12 mm+ timber/ply/OSB/fence board. Doesn't need to be treated — even untreated softwood lasts 10+ years buried, enough for the Type 1 to consolidate into surrounding soil.
  • Hold in place with stakes driven into pit-wall soil, or wedge tight from inside while compacting.
  • Confined Type 1 compacts much harder than unconfined — all the energy goes into densification instead of lateral spread.
Inside the box (manifold / valve / meter support):
  • The manifold body sits on its own internal bricks (~65 mm) raised off the box floor — keeps fittings out of any residual standing water and gives spanner clearance.
  • After commissioning, optionally pour 50 mm of pea gravel in the box bottom around the bricks as a sump.
Pipe entry holes:
  • 35 mm hole saw for 25 mm MDPE — leaves a 5 mm radial gap, perfect for sealing.
  • Cut from outside-in at low RPM (~500–800) with light pressure; deburr both faces.
  • Position the hole at the height the pipe naturally enters — usually 50–150 mm above the box floor, matching the manifold inlet centreline.
  • Pipe runs horizontally for at least 100 mm before entering the wall (no curve at the seal point).
  • Don't seal yet — leave the 5 mm gap open until after pressure testing. Final seal: low-expansion foam (e.g. Soudal Window & Door) or outdoor silicone.
Pipe rise approaching each box:
  • Trench pipe is at –400 mm depth; box wall knockout is ~–200 to –250 mm. The pipe rises over the last ~1 m of trench to meet the box.
  • Max rise gradient: 1 in 10 (e.g. 100 mm rise over 1 m run). Tighter than that and 25 mm MDPE risks kinking.
  • Support the rise with compacted pea gravel mounded under the pipe — or a brick laid flat — to maintain the angle. Sand alone slumps; soil alone washes.
Order of work per box: (1) build Type 1 base inside shutter, compact in lifts → (2) sand levelling + pea gravel + bricks → (3) sit box, check lid at grade → (4) cut pipe entry holes → (5) dry-fit pipes through holes → (6) connect manifold / meter / valve inside box → (7) pressure-test whole system → (8) foam/silicone the pipe entries → (9) backfill pit around boxes (sides only — Type 1 base stays untouched) with native soil + finish to grade.
Never let the box lid sit below grade. Even 5 mm low and every rainstorm runs soil + water over the rim into the box. Aim for flush or +5 mm proud. Surrounding soil/turf should be finished to meet the lid edge cleanly.

6. Hand-dig zones (mini-digger OFF)

Do NOT use the digger bucket:
  • Within 500 mm of the mains gas entry (if applicable — Leo to confirm location)
  • Within 500 mm of the electric meter / main cable entry
  • Within the root plate of any mature tree (typically 1 m radius from trunk per 10 cm trunk diameter)
  • Any visible drain runs, inspection chambers, or soakaway lines
  • Path crossings — lift slabs first, trench by hand under
