1 · Executive Summary

Proposed name: VerdantIQ (alternatives: SmartTurf, Garden Circuit, Hydralux Gardens)

Mission: To bring the convenience, efficiency and aesthetics of smart-home technology into the British garden — designing and installing integrated systems that water, light, mow and secure outdoor spaces automatically.

Services

Smart irrigation, smart garden lighting, robotic mowers and outdoor security cameras — sold as individual projects or as a full integrated Smart Garden package, with ongoing maintenance contracts.

Target Market

Owner-occupied detached and large semi-detached homes across Surrey. Initial focus: Coulsdon, Reigate, Chipstead, Purley, Caterham, Banstead. Typical customer: professional household, income £100k+, garden 100–500 m², already invested in indoor smart-home tech.

Revenue Model

  • Project revenue (design, supply, install): ~80% of Year 1 turnover
  • Recurring revenue (commissioning, winterisation, call-outs, monitoring): grows from 5% → 30%+ by Year 3
  • Modest margin on hardware supply via trade accounts

Funding: Bootstrappable by a founder with £20k personal capital, or £25k Start Up Loan. No equity dilution required — margins and growth profile suit a lifestyle-to-SME business, not a venture-scale play.

Founder Credibility

The founder has personally designed and self-installed a full smart garden at a residential property in Coulsdon, Surrey:

  • Property: Two-section garden (~229 m² irrigable), 1 m height difference, retaining wall
  • Irrigation: 5-zone system, Hunter Pro-HC Hydrawise controller, 16 Pro-S PRS40 heads with MP Rotators, Grundfos SB 3-45 AW pump, 1,000 L Enduramaxx tank — ~£2,668 materials
  • Lighting: Philips Hue outdoor (spotlights, LightStrip, Neon, Festavia) across upper and lower zones
  • Mower & security: Integrated in the design with IP65/66 outdoor power infrastructure

This hands-on expertise — gradient pressure compensation, mixed nozzle precipitation, tank sizing, pipe sleeves through retaining walls, WiFi scheduling — is the credibility foundation that competitors cannot fake or acquire quickly.

2 · Market Opportunity

Market Context

The UK smart garden segment sits at the intersection of three growing markets:

  • UK smart home market — estimated c. £5–6bn in 2025, forecast to grow ~9–11% annually (Statista, IBISWorld — approximate)
  • UK domestic irrigation market — small but expanding post-2022 hosepipe bans and warmer summers
  • Outdoor security cameras — one of the fastest-growing smart home categories in the UK
There is no reliable standalone figure for "UK smart garden services" — the category is so new it is not yet broken out by market research firms. That is itself the opportunity.

4 · Operations Plan

Year 1 — Solo Founder

Founder handles: enquiries, surveys, design, quoting, all irrigation install, low-voltage lighting, robot mower setup, camera mounting & cable runs, commissioning, customer training.

Subcontracted:

  • Part P electrical (mains outlets, fuse-board mods) — local electrician @ £320–£400/day
  • WaterSafe plumber for backflow + mains connection — £180–£280/job
  • Groundworks (long trenches, retaining wall penetrations) — £150–£180/day
Realistic capacity: one small install per week + one medium install per fortnight ≈ 35 install-weeks × £4,500 avg = revenue ceiling ~£150k at full booking. Year 1 plan targets conservative ~£85k.

6 · Marketing & Sales

Channel Priorities

  1. Google Business Profile + local SEO — "smart irrigation Surrey", "robot mower installer Reigate", "garden lighting Coulsdon". Budget: ~£80/month.
  2. Instagram & TikTok — before/after reels, time-lapse installs, "what's inside a valve box" explainers. Tech-adjacent audience consumes this heavily.
  3. YouTube long-form — founder documents the proof-of-concept install. Ranks on "Hunter Pro-HC setup", "Grundfos SB install". Durable inbound moat competitors can't replicate quickly.
  4. Nextdoor & community Facebook groups — high conversion in Surrey commuter belt; word-of-mouth capital.
  5. Partnerships with:
    • Garden designers (want smart tech but can't install)
    • Landscapers (hard landscaping but not irrigation)
    • Builders / renovation firms (new-build integration)
    • Smart-home AV installers (they own the house; you own the garden)
    Referral fee: 7–10% of net project value.
  6. Hero case study — fully documented founder's garden (123 Downs, Coulsdon): schematic, component list, time-lapse, 6-month results. Landing-page centrepiece.
  7. Referral incentive: £150 John Lewis voucher per converting referral.

Sales Process

Enquiry → Qualifying call → Paid site survey (credited) → Written design + quote → 40% deposit → Install window booked → Commissioning + training → 12-month soft review for maintenance upsell

A leaner £12–15k variant is possible (cheaper van, no stock float) but slows the first 6 months.

Break-even

At ~35% gross margin and fixed monthly overheads of ~£1,800 (van, insurance, software, SEO, phone, accountant), break-even = ~£5,100/month revenue ≈ one £5k install per month. Achievable from month 3 with basic marketing.

Funding Strategy

  • Bootstrappable by founder with £20k personal capital
  • Alternative: Start Up Loans Company (£25k @ c. 6.2% APR, 5-year term)
  • No equity investment needed — margins and growth profile don't warrant VC. Rejecting dilution is the right call.

8 · Risks & Mitigations

RiskLikelihoodImpactMitigation
Seasonality cash-flow crunch (Jan–Feb) High Medium Price maintenance contracts to fall in Q1; sell Festavia/lighting Oct–Dec; hold 3 months overhead reserve
Water Regulations non-compliance (fluid-cat-5 backflow) Medium High Always subcontract backflow to WaterSafe plumber until founder gains own certification
Part P electrical breach (DIY mains work) Medium High Never touch consumer-unit / new-circuit work without Part P sign-off; document every job
GDPR/ICO breaches on camera installs Medium Medium Standard privacy pack per customer; ICO registered; signage templates; never aim at neighbour property or public highway
Competitor entry (landscaper adds smart-tech division) Medium Medium Build content + brand moat; lock in repeat customers with maintenance contracts
Supply chain (Hunter/Grundfos lead times) Medium Medium Diversify 2–3 trade suppliers; hold critical-part stock float
Injury / illness (sole-trader single point of failure) Medium High Income protection insurance; documented processes for subcontractor cover
Customer non-payment Low Medium 40% deposit, 40% on install day, 20% on completion; on-site card payment
Hardware warranty claim costs Low Medium Install only products with robust UK trade warranties; pass to customer where possible

9 · 90-Day Action Plan

Days 1–30 · Foundation

  • Register limited company (Companies House, ~£50)
  • Open business bank account (Starling / Tide / Mettle)
  • Quote and purchase insurance (PL, PI, Tools, Van)
  • Register with ICO (£40)
  • Apply for BIA membership
  • Meet 2 Part P electricians + 1 WaterSafe plumber
  • Buy used van; fit out basic racking
  • Finalise brand name, logo, domain, website wireframe

Days 31–60 · Build

  • Website live (services + founder case study)
  • Google Business Profile live with proof-of-concept photography
  • Instagram + YouTube launched — 5 posts, 2 videos minimum
  • Open trade accounts with 3 key suppliers
  • Price list & written design-proposal template finalised
  • Standard contracts drafted (quote acceptance, maintenance, GDPR)
  • First 3 pilot customers lined up — 15–20% discount for case-study rights

Days 61–90 · Trade

  • Complete pilot install #1 (small package, low-risk, fast)
  • Publish first case-study content from pilot
  • Complete pilot install #2 (medium package)
  • Build paid-customer pipeline for spring peak (Mar–Apr)
  • Review unit economics — adjust pricing if margins tight
  • Book spring marketing push (local press, Nextdoor, Instagram ads)
Closing note: This plan intentionally models a sustainable lifestyle-SME, not a venture rocket ship. The business is defensible because it sits at a genuine under-served intersection; it's cash-generative from Year 1; and its founder has rare hands-on credibility that competitors cannot fake. The proof-of-concept garden at 123 Downs, Coulsdon is not just a home — it's the most cost-effective marketing asset this business will ever own.
